## Purpose

Volume 10 defines **how FT ERP evolves and endures** after Volumes 0–9 establish **what it is**, **how it is validated**, and **how it is operated**.

This volume governs **roadmap decisions**, **innovation**, **change control**, **ADRs**, **quality sustainability**, **knowledge preservation**, **documentation governance**, and **long-term stewardship** — ensuring continuous evolution strengthens the product without compromising foundations ([Constitution Art. 23](../01_Product_Foundation/Chapter_02_FT_ERP_Constitution.md)).

Volume 11 (optional, planned) addresses **Manufacturing Knowledge** — domain depth for BOM, RM, and shop-floor patterns.

## Authority

Volume 10 **governs evolution** of the product defined in Volumes 0–9. It does **not** override:

- Constitution (Volume 1)
- Workflow semantics (Volume 4) without formal amendment
- Protected behaviors (Volume 8, FT-PD-081)
- Certification requirements (Volume 8)

---

## Core principle

**Evolution strengthens architecture. It never erodes it.**

- **Roadmap** commits only after architectural impact assessment.
- **Every architectural change** requires an ADR (ADR-01).
- **Documentation** is a governed product asset (KNW-01).
- **Stewardship** preserves customer trust across generations (STW-03).
- **Release** requires certification, health attestation, and aligned documentation before customer GA.
